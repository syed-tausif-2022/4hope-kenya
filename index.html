Our Mission

Built from Nothing.
For Everyone.

4Hope Kenya was founded after watching a previous program collapse due to mismanagement — while orphanage students in Nairobi kept showing up every week asking when the bikes would come. We could not walk away.

We deliver mobile-first engineering education to 250+ students because every lesson works on a phone. No computers. No laptops. Just a phone and the will to learn.

"These aren't statistics. These are real kids with real dreams. They just need the right people in their corner." — Syed Rahman, Founder

Who We Serve

250+ Students.
Two Orphanages.
One Mission.

Children's Garden Home in Nairobi has cared for over 2,000 children since 2001. Today nearly 250 children are in care — sleeping on torn mattresses, facing food insecurity, with no access to computers or engineering education.

Less than 10% of Kenyans own transportation. A bike is survival infrastructure — water collection, school access, safety at night.

Student Story
"I fixed the weld myself. Like you taught me."

Ferdinand called at 2AM from Kenya. After learning to fix it himself, he called back three months later to say he had just taught a younger kid the same repair. That is why 4Hope exists.
