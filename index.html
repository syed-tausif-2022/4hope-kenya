The Founder

Built by a Student.
For Students.

My name is Syed Rahman — a senior at Stuyvesant High School in Queens, NYC. I am a first-generation, low-income student who founded 4Hope Kenya after watching a previous program collapse due to mismanagement while orphanage students in Nairobi kept showing up every week asking when the bikes would come.

I could not walk away. So I built something new from nothing. No funding. No institution. No permission. Just WhatsApp, 8 volunteer engineers, and a commitment to finish what we started.

"These aren't statistics. These are real kids with real dreams. They just need the right people in their corner."

Syed Rahman — Founder, 4Hope Kenya | Stuyvesant High School '26

Our Mission

Built from Nothing.
For Everyone.

4Hope Kenya delivers mobile-first engineering education to orphanage students in Nairobi — because every lesson works on a phone. No computers. No laptops. Just a phone and the will to learn.

We are simultaneously engineering a mountain bike specifically built for Kenyan terrain — rough roads, 00 budget, repairable with basic tools by the teenagers we teach.

📱

Every lesson works on a phone. Accessible to every student regardless of resources.

Who We Serve

250+ Students.
Two Orphanages.
One Mission.

Children's Garden Home in Nairobi has cared for over 2,000 children since 2001. Today nearly 250 children are in care — sleeping on torn mattresses, facing food insecurity, with no access to computers or engineering education.

Less than 10% of Kenyans own transportation. A bike is not recreation. It is survival infrastructure — water collection, school access, safety at night.

Student Story
"I fixed the weld myself. Like you taught me."

Ferdinand called at 2AM from Kenya. His brake cable was jammed. After learning to fix it himself, he called back three months later — not for help, but to say he had just taught a younger kid the same repair. That is why 4Hope exists.
